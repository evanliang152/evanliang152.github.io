Political Systems and Structures

Analyzing the Senate and House of Representatives, powers of Congress, media's role in the Democratic Processes, and the checks and balances of the United States government

The Senate

Structure and Composition

  • 100 members, 2 per state - historic aspects of New Jersey Plan
  • 6 year terms, â…“ of members elected every 2 years
  • Must be at least 30 years old, citizen for 9 years and live in the state they represent
  • Filibuster allowed - procedural rule requiring 60 votes to end debate

Special Powers

  • Carry out impeachment trial after Representatives have impeached the president
  • Choose Vice President if there is no Electoral College winner
  • Ratify Treaties
  • Less committee-based, more bipartisanship, consensus and consent

Constitutional Changes

17th Amendment (1913) - Established direct election of senators by popular vote. Previously, senators were chosen by state legislatures.

House of Representatives

Structure and Composition

  • 435 members, proportional representation - historically derived from Virginia Plan
  • Each member represents approximately 760,000 constituents
  • Must be at least 25 years old and live in the state of their district
  • Led by the Speaker of the House

Special Powers

  • Write impeachment cases against federal officials
  • Choose President if no winner in Electoral College
  • All revenue bills must originate in the House

Operating Style

Committee-based structure, more partisan, majority rules, with formal rules and decorum emphasized.

Powers of Congress

Legislative Powers
  • Write and pass legislation
  • Necessary and Proper Clause
  • Override presidential vetoes with 2/3 majority
Financial Powers
  • Lay and collect taxes
  • Borrow money
  • Commerce Clause
  • Coin money and regulate its value
Other Powers
  • Declare War
  • Raise and maintain military forces
  • Immigration and Naturalization
  • Control over District of Columbia

Checks and Balances

Executive Branch Checks

  • President can veto legislation
  • President appoints Supreme Court justices
  • President serves as Commander-in-Chief

Legislative Branch Checks

  • Congress can impeach the president
  • Congress approves federal judges
  • Senate confirms presidential appointments
  • Congress can override presidential vetoes

Judicial Branch Checks

  • Supreme Court interprets laws
  • Supreme Court can declare laws unconstitutional
  • Judicial review established in Marbury v. Madison

Media's Role in Democratic Processes

Media Influence

Media significantly influences public opinion, presenting information either with full context or through selective clips. The case of Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene demonstrates how media coverage can shape public perception of political figures.

Media platforms can be leveraged to mobilize constituents around specific issues or amplify politicians' messages to broader audiences.

Incumbency Advantage

Current elected officials often benefit from established media relationships and coverage, contributing to the "incumbency effect" that makes it easier for sitting officials to retain their positions.

Media coverage can create name recognition and familiarity, which are significant factors in electoral success.
